Managing Multiple Domains

Strategies for Multi-Domain Management

Organizations often manage dozens or hundreds of domain names for different brands, products, regions, and defensive registrations. Effective management prevents costly oversights and security risks.

Why Organizations Own Multiple Domains

  • Brand protection: Registering variations to prevent typosquatting
  • Multiple TLDs: Securing .com, .net, .org, and ccTLDs
  • Product brands: Separate domains for different product lines
  • Regional presence: Country-specific domains for international markets
  • Marketing campaigns: Short, memorable domains for promotions
  • Investment: Domain portfolios held for future sale or use

Domain Portfolio Management

Centralized Inventory

  • Maintain a master spreadsheet or use domain management software
  • Track: domain name, registrar, expiration date, DNS provider, purpose, owner/department
  • Review and update at least quarterly

Registrar Consolidation

  • Transfer domains to as few registrars as possible
  • Use enterprise registrar accounts for volume discounts
  • Choose registrars with strong APIs for automation
  • Ensure the registrar supports all your TLDs

DNS Management

  • Use a centralized DNS provider for all domains
  • Implement Infrastructure as Code for DNS (e.g., Terraform, OctoDNS)
  • Version control your DNS configurations
  • Use templates for common configurations

Security for Domain Portfolios

  • Enable transfer lock on all domains
  • Use registry lock for high-value domains
  • Implement role-based access control
  • Use a dedicated, secured email for domain management
  • Enable 2FA on all registrar accounts
  • Audit access permissions regularly

Automation and Tools

  • Registrar APIs: Automate renewal, DNS changes, and monitoring
  • Domain management platforms: CSC, MarkMonitor, Gandi Corporate
  • Monitoring services: Track expiration, DNS changes, and SSL status
  • WHOIS monitoring: Alert on unauthorized changes

Cost Optimization

  • Regularly review your portfolio — drop domains you no longer need
  • Register important domains for multiple years for discounts
  • Negotiate volume pricing with your registrar
  • Evaluate whether defensive registrations are still necessary
