How to Check Domain Age & Why It Matters

Understanding Domain Age

Domain age refers to how long a domain name has been registered and active. It's an important metric for SEO analysis, domain investment, trust evaluation, and competitive research.

Why Domain Age Matters

SEO Impact

  • Older domains may have accumulated backlinks and authority over time
  • Google's algorithms consider domain history as a trust signal
  • New domains often go through a "sandbox" period with limited rankings
  • Domain age alone doesn't guarantee rankings — content and links matter more

Trust and Credibility

  • Older domains are generally perceived as more trustworthy
  • Newly registered domains are more commonly associated with spam or phishing
  • Financial institutions and security tools flag very new domains

Domain Investment

  • Older domains with clean histories command higher prices
  • Age contributes to perceived authority and value
  • Historical backlink profiles accumulated over years add significant value

How to Check Domain Age

WHOIS Lookup

The most reliable method — check the "Creation Date" or "Registered On" field in WHOIS results.

  • Use our WHOIS lookup tool to instantly check any domain's creation date
  • Also shows registration, update, and expiration dates
  • Some ccTLDs may not expose creation dates

Internet Archive (Wayback Machine)

Shows when a domain was first crawled and what content it hosted over time. Useful when WHOIS history is limited.

Domain Age vs. Index Age

  • Registration age: When the domain was first registered (WHOIS creation date)
  • Index age: When Google first crawled and indexed the domain
  • Content age: When meaningful content was first published
  • A domain registered in 2005 but only developed in 2024 has limited SEO benefit from age

Red Flags in Domain Age Analysis

  • Domain age resets if it expires and is re-registered by a new owner
  • Recently dropped and re-registered domains may carry penalties
  • Check for gaps in registration history that indicate the domain was dropped
  • Verify the domain wasn't previously used for spam or malware
