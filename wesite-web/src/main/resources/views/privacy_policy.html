1. Information We Collect

When you use Whose.Domains, we may collect the following types of information to provide and improve our services:

Information You Provide Directly

This includes information you voluntarily submit through our website forms, such as:

  • Domain names you enter for WHOIS lookup
  • Messages sent through our "Contact Us" form
  • Email addresses provided when subscribing to notifications

Automatically Collected Technical Information

When you browse our site, we automatically collect certain anonymous information through cookies and similar technologies. This information generally cannot be used to directly identify you and includes:

  • IP Address: Used for approximate geolocation (typically to city level) to display localized relevant information and to protect website security.
  • Device and Browser Information: Such as your device type, browser version, and operating system, to optimize how the website displays on your device.
  • Usage Data: Including pages you visit, time spent on pages, links clicked, and referral sources.

WHOIS Query Data

We may log the domain names you query and the time of query to analyze global domain registration trends and generate anonymous, aggregated statistics. This usage data is not linked to your personal identity.

2. How We Use Your Information

Purpose Description

Communication If you subscribed to our emails, we will use the email address you provided to send updates (you can unsubscribe at any time).

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell or rent your personal data. We only share information with third parties in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We work with third-party service providers (such as hosting and analytics services) bound by contractual obligations. They may only use your information for the purpose of providing services to us and for no other purpose.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information when we believe in good faith that it is necessary to comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, or enforceable governmental requests.
  • With Your Consent: We will share information with third parties when we have your explicit consent to do so.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies to enhance your browsing experience.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by websites to remember your preferences and settings.

How You Can Control Them?

Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. You can manage this through your browser settings, but please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some parts of this website.

5. Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access and Correction: You can contact us to ask what personal data we hold about you and to request correction of inaccurate information.
  • Objection to Processing: You can object to our use of your personal data for certain purposes, such as direct marketing.
  • Data Portability: You can request a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable copy of the personal data you have provided to us.
  • Deletion: In certain circumstances, you can ask us to erase your personal data.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided at the end of this policy.
